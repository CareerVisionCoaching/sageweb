The Origin Story

I'm Josh Bob, and before building Sage, I spent 4+ years as a career coach helping mid-career tech professionals land their dream jobs at companies like Google, Amazon, HubSpot, Meta, Reddit, and dozens of fast-growth startups.

I personally coached 150+ clients through 1,000+ sessions. I reviewed thousands of resumes and LinkedIn profiles. I guided people through career transitions, helped them negotiate offers, and celebrated when they landed roles they were proud of.

But here's the thing: the tools I was using weren't built for what I was doing.

The Breaking Point

Three clients in the same week missed deadlines because each thought they were waiting on the other. I realized: if I, someone obsessed with structure, can't keep it straight — how is any other coach supposed to scale this?

"Sage wasn't born in a brainstorm; it was born in frustration."

I was managing clients across Notion, Google Sheets, Calendly, Gmail, Drive, and Slack. Every tool worked fine on its own, but nothing talked to each other. I spent 5-10 hours a week just moving information around, tracking who needed what, and trying to remember where I left that one client's updated resume.

Generic CRMs were built for sales pipelines, not career transformations. Practice management tools were designed for wellness coaches, not job search journeys. And cobbling together Notion templates only worked until I hit 20+ clients.

So I built Sage — first for myself, then for coaches like me.

Built in the Trenches

Every feature in Sage comes from real coaching experience:

  • Visual roadmaps — because clients need to see their progress, not just hear about it
  • Review requests — because tracking resume versions in email threads is chaos
  • Automated reminders — because follow-up shouldn't take an hour every Sunday
  • Analytics — because "I think this is working" isn't good enough

I used early prototypes in my own coaching practice before offering them to anyone else. Client completion rates went from 78% to 91%. Review turnaround dropped by 50%. I saved 8-10 hours per week. Most importantly: my clients felt more confident and less lost during their search.
